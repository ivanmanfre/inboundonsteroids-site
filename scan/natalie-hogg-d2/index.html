The clarity problem no one's talking about

If AI can't say what you do, your buyers probably can't either.

Six months in, what are you willing to stop?

The Dentsu CMO Navigator from January said almost half of CMOs aren't confident their teams can pull off the strategies they say matter most. Six months in, that gap hasn't closed on its own. You don't fix it by adding more. You fix it by cutting what you can't prove is working, and putting that money behind what you can.

Your AI problem is a clarity problem

A study recently found that AI models describe the same B2B companies in contradictory ways 28% of the time. The model knows who you are. It's much less sure what you actually do. That confusion didn't start with AI. If you put your features and pricing in a clean, machine-readable format, AI cites you about three times more often. The same clarity that gets you cited is what moves a buyer to choose you.

Signals are faster now. Judgment isn't.

AI surfaces more signals and helps you rule out the dead ones quicker. What it doesn't do is make the call. Knowing which signal actually matters to your business is judgment, not information. The people worth hiring aren't the ones who build models anymore. They're the ones who know the business well enough to ask the right question.

Day 1

Your Signal Audit: start with question two

The audit is set up so you can work through it in one sitting. Day 4

The channel question in the audit

Question one asks which channels look strong in the dashboard but haven't moved a real number in 90 days. The tricky part is that good-looking metrics make this question feel answered when it isn't. Before you move on from it, check whether the channel has driven a trackable lead or sale in the last quarter. Traffic and engagement count; revenue movement is the actual signal.
