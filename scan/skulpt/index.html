Early-stage founders show me a feature list and ask why nobody converts. The list is fine. It just answers a question the buyer isn't asking yet. Before people care what your product does, they decide what it says about them to use it.

Oatly didn't win the fridge shelf with oat-to-liquid ratios. They won it by making a carton feel like a point of view.

Meaning is the part most startups skip, because it can't be spec'd on a slide. It's also the part people pay extra for.

If your brand only describes the product, you're left competing on price. Give people something to belong to.
